Contract Law Fundamentals Legal Reference
Formation Requirements: Offer, Acceptance, Consideration, Capacity, and Legality
Offer: Definite proposal communicated to offeree with intent to be bound
Acceptance: Unconditional agreement to all material terms; counter-offer terminates original offer
Consideration: Something of legal value exchanged by both parties; past consideration invalid
Statute of Frauds: Contracts over one year, real estate, goods over $500 must be in writing
Breach of Contract: Material breach allows non-breaching party to treat contract as terminated
Damages: Compensatory, Consequential, Punitive (rare), Nominal
Specific Performance: Equitable remedy requiring breaching party to perform
Force Majeure: Clause excusing performance due to extraordinary events beyond party control
Liquidated Damages: Pre-agreed damage amount for breach; enforceable if reasonable
Indemnification: One party agrees to hold other harmless from specified losses
Limitation of Liability: Caps damages recoverable; enforceable except for gross negligence
