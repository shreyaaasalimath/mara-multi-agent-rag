Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models
Authors: Kaplan, McCandlish, Henighan, Brown et al
Institution: OpenAI, 2020
Performance of language models follows smooth power laws with model size, dataset size, and compute
Larger models are significantly more sample efficient than smaller models
Optimal compute allocation: equal scaling of model size and training tokens
Model size, dataset size, and compute budget are the three key scaling variables
Doubling model size while halving training steps often outperforms smaller model trained longer
Architectural details like depth vs width matter less than total parameter count
These findings influenced GPT-3 (175B), PaLM (540B), and Chinchilla scaling decisions
Chinchilla paper refined these laws: optimal token-to-parameter ratio of 20:1
